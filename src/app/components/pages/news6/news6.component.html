Two Twin Students from Katingan Win International Competition

The hot sun did not make the twin brothers Raynando Kurniawan and Kristian Reynaldy to break charcoal in pursuit of their dreams. The sons of Katingan Regency, Raynando Kurniawan and Kristian Reynaldy, who went to Surabaya, East Java, have successfully made Penyang Hinje Simpei proud.


These two students of grade XI Science 1 SMA Negeri 1 Kasongan won silver medals or second place. They won a special award from Malaysia as the best research in the 2022 National Applied Science Project Olympiad (NASPO) research competition.


"This activity was held on December 14 to 17, 2022, in Surabaya and organized by the Indonesian Young Scientist Association (IYSA) in collaboration with the Actuarial Department of the Sepuluh Nopember Institute of Technology, Surabaya," said Reynaldi, Monday (19/12/2022).


Raynando Kurniawan and Kristian Reynaldy's research entitled "Legend Innovation" raised the story of Dayak land as a regional preservation. This research aims to prevent folklore or legends in Dayak land from becoming extinct with this research so that the younger generation in Indonesia knows the folklore or legend that exists in Indonesia.


"This research can encourage literacy interest among the wider community," said Kristian. The man with glasses said that nervousness had haunted him and his twin brother when competing in ISYA. "The feeling of fast heartbeat is inevitable, but it can be overcome with confidence and not inferior or embarrassed by other countries," he said.


NASPO and I2ASPO are annual events held by ISYA at the end of each year. Moreover, this activity was attended by 17 countries in Asia and Europe.
